### **Feasibility Study on Legalizing Holdback Periods: Analyzing Utility in the Korean Film Industry (2025)**

Proposed a **'Dynamic Holdback Matrix'** by analyzing theater revenue data, demonstrating that rigid regulations reduce total industry utility. Suggested a flexible windowing strategy based on platform compatibility to maximize revenue.

**Role: Research Lead & Data Analyst (Python, LSTM Modeling)**

- Designed a system to evaluate platform compatibility (Theater vs. OTT) based on genre characteristics.
- Built a **Stacked LSTM model** using KOBIS data to predict daily audience decay rates and the cannibalization effect of OTT release timing.
- Quantified that prolonged holdback periods favor theaters and distributors.
- Defined a total industry utility function and proved that uniform regulations lower this utility, proposing a dynamic policy to maximize it.

**Key Result:**

- 3rd Place, Data Analytics Academic Conference

**Key Takeaways:**

- **[Data-Driven Decision Modeling]** Overcame the limitations of simple data analysis by integrating business logic (profitability). Acquired the capability to simulate practical strategies by combining industry data (KOBIS) with market variables (OTT cannibalization).
- **[Regulatory Risk Analysis]** Quantified the financial impact of market regulations (Holdback legalization) on business models, demonstrating the ability to propose alternatives that minimize regulatory risk.
